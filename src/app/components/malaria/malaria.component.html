Malaria is a life-threatening disease transmitted through the bite of infected female mosquitoes. It is most common in tropical regions, particularly in Africa. The disease is caused by parasites that multiply in the liver and infect red blood cells, leading to recurring cycles of fever, chills, and flu-like symptoms. Severe cases can result in organ failure and death, especially in vulnerable populations. Diagnosis is done through blood tests, and treatment involves specific antimalarial medications. Prevention measures include using bed nets, indoor spraying, and taking prophylactic medications when traveling to affected areas. Public health efforts to control mosquitoes and promote early diagnosis are crucial in combating malaria.
