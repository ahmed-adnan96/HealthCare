Pneumonia is an infection that causes inflammation in the air sacs of the lungs, leading to fluid or pus accumulation. It can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or fungi and is typically spread through respiratory droplets. Symptoms include cough, fever, chest pain, difficulty breathing, fatigue, and confusion. Diagnosis involves physical examination, chest X-rays, and laboratory tests. Treatment depends on the cause and severity and may include antibiotics, antivirals, or supportive care. Prevention measures include vaccinations, hand hygiene, and avoiding respiratory infections. Early detection and prompt treatment are crucial for managing pneumonia effectively and preventing complications.
