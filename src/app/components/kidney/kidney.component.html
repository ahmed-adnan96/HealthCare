Kidney disease occurs when the kidneys are damaged and cannot function properly. Causes include diabetes, high blood pressure, genetic factors, infections, or certain medications. It can progress through various stages, leading to complications such as chronic kidney disease (CKD), kidney failure, or end-stage renal disease (ESRD) requiring dialysis or transplantation. Symptoms may include fatigue, swelling, changes in urine output, high blood pressure, and electrolyte imbalances. Early detection, managing underlying conditions, adopting a healthy lifestyle, and medication can help slow the progression of kidney disease. Treatment options for advanced stages may include dialysis or transplantation. Education about kidney health, maintaining a balanced diet, staying hydrated, and avoiding nephrotoxic substances are important for prevention and effective management of kidney disease.
